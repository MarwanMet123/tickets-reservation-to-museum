Farming tools in ancient Egypt

Ancient Egyptian farmers used a number of tools to work the soil of the Nile Delta. Some of these are in use today, like hoes, sickles, hand plows, pitchforks and sieves. A lesser-known tool called the shaduf, still used in some parts of the world, was important for irrigation.

Carpentry tools of the ancient Egyptians

During the earliest pre-dynastic period (circa 3100 B.C., about the time of the first pharaoh), they also used mortise and tenon joints to join pieces of wood. Pegs, dowels, and leather or cord lashings strengthened these joints. Animal glue was used during the New Kingdom period (1570 – 1069 B.C.).
